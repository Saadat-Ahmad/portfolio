# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Hiring managers, engineering leads, and recruiters evaluating Syed Saadat Ahmad (full-stack & systems engineer, AMU Computer Engineering '27) for roles or collaboration. They arrive with limited time and high skepticism, often on a laptop, sometimes on mobile. The job to be done: quickly judge whether this person is technically strong, has real shipped work, and has taste. A secondary audience is peers and collaborators who appreciate craft for its own sake.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio that proves competence through the artifact itself, not just claims about it. It exists to convert a skeptical visitor into "I want to talk to this person" inside a couple of minutes. Success looks like: the work and experience read clearly, the craft of the site signals engineering taste, and contact is frictionless. The site is the work sample.

## Brand Personality

Quiet-editorial meets Swiss-brutalist, with retro-computing flavor. Three words: precise, warm, characterful. Voice is confident and plainspoken, never salesy or buzzword-laden. The interface should feel like a well-set printed object (typographic, gridded, deliberate) with a wink of classic-Macintosh personality. It should evoke calm confidence and a little delight, never urgency or hype.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS / startup landing pages: Inter-everywhere, purple gradients, hero-metric templates, identical icon-card grids.
- Dark "developer terminal" cliché as a costume.
- Glassmorphism, gradient text, neon glow, bounce/elastic motion.
- Over-animated scroll-jacking that fights the reader.
- Anything that reads as templated or AI-generated. The site should look made, not assembled.

## Design Principles

- **The site is the proof.** Craft of the page is itself evidence of engineering taste. Practice what you preach.
- **Quiet confidence over loud claims.** Show shipped work and let restraint signal seniority. No shouting type, no hype copy.
- **Typographic and gridded.** Fraunces display + Space Mono labels + Inter body, thin rules, deliberate rhythm. Type carries the design.
- **Characterful, not decorative.** The classic-Mac window chrome and ASCII touches are signature motifs used with intent, not ornament sprinkled everywhere.
- **Legible first.** Warmth and personality never cost readability or accessibility.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text ≥ 4.5:1 against its surface (note: panel text on sage/brick was deliberately deepened to keep cream text AA-legible). Visible keyboard focus on all interactive elements. Full `prefers-reduced-motion` support (reveals, marquee, hero entrance, glitch, cursor blink all already gated). Custom cursor is desktop fine-pointer only and must never remove the affordance on touch. Respect color-blind safety: never rely on the orange/sage/brick hues alone to convey meaning.
